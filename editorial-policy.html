Editorial Policy

steamgames.fun is built as a player-first guide hub, not a scraped store directory. Store pages give baseline facts; the guide value comes from original route planning, decision tables, beginner mistakes, and update notes.

What makes a guide publishable

Useful decisions

A page should help a player choose a route, build, role, resource priority, or first-session plan.

Source baseline

Release dates, developers, publishers, tags, and feature claims are checked against Steam or official sources when possible.

No placeholder cards

Games stay off the public guide library until there is enough original strategy text to justify a reader's click.

Early Access and live games

Many Steam games change quickly. The site uses update dates, source notes, and cautious language when a mechanic may shift after a patch. Exact tier lists are avoided until the community has enough live data; stable beginner habits are prioritized over fragile claims.

When a patch changes a core system, older pages are updated or clarified. Corrections can be sent through the contact page with the game version, page URL, and the section that needs review.

Advertising boundaries

The site may use Google AdSense. Advertising does not determine which games are covered, and the site does not ask readers to click ads. Policy pages and guide pages are written to be useful even without advertising.
