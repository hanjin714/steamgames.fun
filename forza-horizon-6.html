Forza Horizon 6 Starter Guide

Forza Horizon 6 moves the Horizon festival to Japan with a large open-world driving map and more than 550 real-world cars. This guide focuses on the first sessions: how to choose cars, unlock event variety, learn the map, and avoid wasting credits early.

Release: May 18, 2026 Developer: Playground Games Publisher: Xbox Game Studios

First Two Hours Route

  1. Drive widely before specializing.Spend the first hour sampling road, dirt, street, and mixed-surface events. The point is not to win every race immediately; it is to learn how the Japan map changes grip, braking distance, and visibility across city streets, mountain roads, and rural routes.
  2. Keep one stable car for each surface.Early credits disappear quickly if you buy cars for every event. Build a small garage: one road car, one dirt/rally option, one all-rounder, and one car you simply enjoy driving. Upgrade only after you know which events you repeat.
  3. Unlock event types before chasing perfect times.Progression is smoother when the map opens up. Complete introductory events, story markers, and showcase-style activities first, then return to optimize lap times once you have more fast travel points and car options.
  4. Use assists as training tools.Start with traction and braking assists if needed, then reduce them gradually. Forza rewards smooth inputs, so the fastest early improvement usually comes from cleaner corner entry, not a more expensive vehicle.

Early Car Strategy

Road car

Pick something predictable rather than extreme. A controllable A or S class road build teaches braking points and racing lines better than an overpowered car that slides through every corner.

Dirt and mixed surface

Prioritize acceleration, stability, and suspension over top speed. Japan's tighter routes punish cars that cannot recover quickly after uneven terrain or sudden turns.

Credit discipline

Avoid buying duplicates in the opening hours. Use reward cars and early unlocks first, then spend credits only when an event category clearly needs a dedicated build.

Upgrade rule

Upgrade tires, brakes, and handling before chasing raw horsepower. A balanced build wins more early events than a fast car that cannot stop or turn.

Practical Driving Notes

On technical roads, brake earlier than your instincts suggest and finish most braking before turn-in. If the car understeers, reduce entry speed; if it oversteers, soften throttle input on exit. For new players, consistency beats spectacle: five clean corners usually gain more time than one perfect drift followed by a crash.

Japan's mountain routes reward drivers who memorize braking markers. Pick a short section of road you enjoy, repeat it with different cars, and use it as a personal benchmark for handling upgrades. The fastest drivers on the leaderboard are not always the ones with the most expensive cars — they are the ones who know every corner of their test loop.

This guide avoids claiming exact car rankings until the live meta settles. Forza games change quickly as players discover tunes, reward routes, and seasonal event patterns. Check community lap times and seasonal leaderboards before committing large credit investments.
